# Voice & Tone Bible 

### Marriage Stabilization & Strengthening System

*Internal working document*

---

## Purpose

This is the writing standard for the whole system.

Every piece of writing gets tested against this document before it ships:

* app UI
* prompts
* tools
* articles
* emails
* scripts
* social posts
* push notifications

If it does not help a tired, hurt person understand what is happening and what to do next, it does not ship.

---

## Internal Creative Standard

**Battle-Tested Medic**

This is for writers, not readers.

A medic does not lecture.
A medic does not panic.
A medic does not pick sides.
A medic reads the scene fast, stops the bleeding, and gives the next clear instruction.

That is the posture behind every word we write.

---

## Public-Facing Voice

We are:

* calm
* plain
* direct
* useful

We sound like the one person in the room who is not making things worse.

Not a therapist.
Not a preacher.
Not a gender-war account.
Not a self-help influencer.
Not a romance fantasy brand.

We sound like someone who understands marriage under strain and knows what comes next.

---

## Core Rule

> **Say what is happening. Say why it matters. Say what to do next.**

Every piece of content must do all three.

If we only name the problem, we add weight.
If we only give advice, we sound generic.
If we explain too much, we lose the room.

---

## Who We Write For

We write for people who are often:

* hurt
* confused
* defensive
* ashamed
* angry
* lonely
* exhausted
* still hoping

They are often reading at the worst time:

* after a fight
* in bed
* in the car
* in silence
* late at night
* when they feel done

Write for that version of them.

---

## Product Boundary

We are for couples with remaining goodwill.

We are not for:

* abuse cover-ups
* coercion
* affair normalisation
* fake neutrality where safety is gone

We are pro-family.
We are pro-repair.
We are anti-ideology.
We do not romanticise dead marriages.
We do not glamorise leaving as an identity.

Remaining goodwill is not enough on its own. If fear, coercion, intimidation, degradation, or repeated boundary violation are present, repair language must not be used to normalise harm.

---

## Gender Rule

**We never make one person’s pain the evidence against the other.**

A man feeling rejected and a woman feeling touched out can both be true in the same marriage.
A woman carrying too much and a man going cold from repeated rejection can both be true in the same marriage.

We do not take the bait.
We do not write for one side against the other.
We name the loop and help people break it.

The moment we sound like we are on one gender’s team, we lose trust.

---

## Emotional Posture

We are not shocked by bad behavior.

We are not casual about pain.

We do not flatter people.

We do not humiliate them either.

We tell the truth in a way people can still use.

The reader should finish our content thinking:

> **These people get it. They are not judging me. They are not wasting my time.**

## Three Writing Modes

### 1. When Someone Is Hurt

Used for:

* betrayal
* cheating
* grief
* deep resentment
* loneliness inside marriage

How it sounds:

* slower
* steadier
* less instruction at first
* more naming

What it does:

* says the thing they are living but not naming
* lowers panic
* protects dignity
* creates room for repair

Examples:

* *You are not overreacting. This hurts because it matters.*
* *You can love someone and still feel yourself going cold.*
* *Relief is data. If you feel lighter when they are gone, that means something.*

---

### 2. When Someone Is Blaming

Used for:

* extreme posts
* hot takes
* gender-war content
* rigid stories
* distorted interpretations

How it sounds:

* clear
* firm
* unsarcastic
* unafraid

What it does:

* finds the truth inside the distortion
* strips out the ideology
* points back to what helps

Examples:

* *There is something real under this anger. Let’s name that first.*
* *Feeling unwanted in your own marriage is brutal. Punishment will not fix it.*
* *This is not proof that all men are selfish or all women are cold. This is what drift looks like when nobody stops it.*

---

### 3. When Someone Needs to Act

Used for:

* tool copy
* repair steps
* app prompts
* push notifications
* scripts

How it sounds:

* short
* specific
* clean
* low-friction

What it does:

* gives one move
* reduces damage
* creates traction

Examples:

* *Do not solve the whole marriage tonight. Lower the temperature first.*
* *No long talk right now. Sit near them. Stay there.*
* *Ask one honest question. Then stop talking.*

---

## Writing Shape

### Sentences

Most sentences should be short.

Good:

* *You are not crazy.*
* *Silence is not always peace.*
* *This went cold slowly.*

Use a longer sentence only when it earns its place.

### Paragraphs

Keep paragraphs short.
One to three lines most of the time.

### Headings

Headings should sound like what the reader is living.

Good:

* *We feel like roommates*
* *I do not want sex, but I do not want to lose us*
* *Everything turns into a fight*
* *I am tired of carrying this alone*

Bad:

* *Relational asymmetry under attachment stress*
* *Erotic polarity collapse*
* *Household incentive failure*

### Lists

Use lists when the reader needs to scan.
Do not use lists to sound organised.

## Words We Use

Use words the audience already thinks in:

* drift
* stuck
* cold
* shut down
* checked out
* touched out
* lonely
* unwanted
* resentful
* roommates
* carrying it alone
* walking on eggshells
* going through the motions
* repair
* reset
* next step
* stop making it worse
* still fixable
* turn toward

These words land fast.
They do not need translating.

---

## Words We Cut

Do not use words that create distance.

| Cut This              | Why                            |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| polarity              | nobody says this at home       |
| incentive loops       | strategy-deck language         |
| attachment needs      | therapy-speak                  |
| dysregulation         | academic and cold              |
| erotic vitality       | sounds fake                    |
| validate / validating | therapist filler               |
| emotionally literate  | condescending tone             |
| asymmetry             | wrong register                 |
| oxytocin / cortisol   | wrong in moments of pain       |
| tonal architecture    | writing-about-writing language |
| just communicate      | insulting and vague            |
| just leave            | not our job, not our brand     |
| you deserve better    | hollow reflex                  |
| narcissist            | overused and usually lazy      |
| real man / real woman | ideology bait                  |

---

## Line We Do Not Cross

We do not write copy that:

* pressures someone into sex
* excuses betrayal
* romanticises contempt
* flatters martyrdom
* turns pain into propaganda
* implies one partner’s pain automatically cancels the other’s
* sounds clever at the cost of sounding human

---

## Controversy Rule

When entering a heated topic:

### Always

1. Name the pain under the take
2. Strip out the distortion
3. Point to the next useful move

### Never

* mock the post
* moralise too early
* flatten one side
* hide behind blandness

Bad:

* *This is toxic. Couples need to communicate better.*

Better:

* *This post is angry because feeling unwanted in your own marriage is brutal. That part is real. The part that will hurt you is treating punishment like repair.*

---

## Product Rule

This is not a floating content brand.
This is a repair system.

Every piece of writing should help the user do at least one of these:

* spot drift
* stop escalation
* understand the loop
* reduce damage
* repair contact
* protect desire
* hold fairness
* keep the home from going cold

If it does none of those, it does not belong here.

---

## Format Rules

### App UI

Use the fewest words possible.
No flourish.

Examples:

* *You are both flooded. Pause this conversation.*
* *Try again in 20 minutes.*

### Push Notifications

One idea only.
No slogans that sound fake.

Good:

* *Tonight is for less damage, not a full fix.*
* *Do not mistake distance for calm. Check in.*

### Articles

Name the pattern early.
Do not spend six paragraphs warming up.
Give the reader traction within the first screen.

### Social Posts

Hook fast.
Do not use cheap outrage.
If the post starts hot, cool it quickly.
If the post names pain, end with a move.

### Emails

Be useful, not cosy for the sake of tone.
Respect time.
Do not sound like a newsletter brand.

## Calibration Lines

*Living standard — update as stronger lines prove themselves in real content.*

These belong in this system:

* *This does not look dead. It looks neglected.*
* *If every reach gets punished, people stop reaching.*
* *You are not trying to win tonight. You are trying to stop more damage.*
* *Being touched out and touch-starved can happen in the same house.*
* *Do not confuse quiet with repair.*
* *If you feel more like a parent than a partner, desire usually drops.*
* *The marriage probably did not break in one moment. It went cold in layers.*
* *What feels small now gets expensive later.*
* *Goodwill is not enough on its own. It has to be used.*

---

## Pre-Publish Test

Before anything ships, ask:

1. Did we say what is actually happening?
2. Did we avoid turning this into a side-taking exercise?
3. Did we use words a tired person would actually read?
4. Did we give a move, not just a thought?
5. Would this help at midnight?

If not, rewrite.

---

## One-Line Brief for Every Writer

> **Write like a calm medic in a struggling marriage: see the wound fast, say it plainly, and give the next useful move.**
