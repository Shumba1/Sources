# SYSTEM_DECAY_REPAIR_MODEL.md

**Status:** Companion doctrine note  
**Authority level:** Reference doctrine companion — does not override `MASTER_SPEC.md` or executable config  
**Purpose:** Preserve the operating model from the raw project doctrine so the product does not lose its commercial and behavioural logic through over-compression

---

## 1. Why this note exists

The project doctrine in `MASTER_SPEC.md` correctly preserves mission, audience, family doctrine, desire doctrine, and repair-first architecture.

What it compresses heavily is the **operating model** that explains:

- what is decaying,
- why that decay matters,
- what the system is actually repairing,
- and why the monetisation engine is symptom-to-protocol conversion rather than abstract wisdom.

This note preserves that operating model without trying to become the executable spec.

---

## 2. The blunt truth

The **System Decay / System Repair** layer is where the app stops being a philosophy repository and becomes a tool people will actually pay for.

Most couples do not need rescue theatre. They need course correction.

The product must help users:

1. detect drift early,
2. stop making it worse,
3. apply the right repair protocol,
4. restore enough bond, desire, and reciprocity to stabilise the family-supporting bond,
5. maintain the system so the relationship does not quietly become the least affirming, least erotic, or least safe place in either partner’s life.

---

## 3. The three-engine model

The system is built around three interacting engines.

### 3.1 Bond engine
What keeps people emotionally accessible to each other.

Includes:
- affection
- reassurance
- touch baseline
- repair after friction
- everyday warmth

### 3.2 Incentive engine
What keeps effort, appreciation, and being chosen from collapsing.

Includes:
- appreciation
- initiation balance
- effort rewarded rather than ignored
- fairness signals
- being chosen rather than merely tolerated

### 3.3 Desire engine
What protects attraction from being flattened by over-familiarity, resentment, logistics, and outside comparison.

Includes:
- novelty
- vitality
- tension
- erotic maintenance
- chosen vs obligated polarity

### 3.4 The governing claim
Most couples fail because bond, incentives, and desire decay **simultaneously** and nobody intervenes early.

---

## 4. The decay questions the product must answer fast

Every strong intervention should make one or more of these questions easier to answer:

1. What is decaying?
2. How bad is it?
3. What makes it worse?
4. What repairs it?
5. How do we keep desire, warmth, and family momentum alive most of the year?

---

## 5. Core maintenance rules

These rules should remain visible across repair content, tools, and monetisation posture.

- every “no” includes repair
- intimacy is never weaponised
- desire is cultivated, not assumed
- each partner should remain attractive to the world, not merely domestically functional
- the relationship must not become the least erotic place in either partner’s life
- problems should be addressed early, calmly, and specifically

---

## 6. Product-safe desire framing

The product promise is not “be horny all the time.”

The practical standard is:

> keep the marriage from becoming the least erotic, least affirming, least safe, or least desired place in either partner’s life.

This is translated into product language through:
- novelty hunger
- external validation risk
- affair vulnerability
- comparison dynamics
- complacency penalties

Never internet ideology.

---

## 7. Symptom-to-protocol conversion is the monetisation engine

The commercial opportunity is not abstract wisdom.
The commercial opportunity is **symptom → protocol → reset → continuity**.

People pay when they can say:
- we keep snapping over nothing
- I don’t want sex but I still want us
- I’ve stopped initiating
- I feel like a manager, not a lover
- we love each other but the chemistry is flat
- we are staying for the kids but the atmosphere is dying

And the system replies:
- here is your state,
- here is your first fix,
- here is your 7-day protocol,
- here is your paid reset if it persists.

---

## 8. Why this note matters

Without this model, the product can still look coherent but lose the logic that makes:
- Repair the real centre,
- desire maintenance credible,
- monetisation native,
- and the whole system feel like an operating system rather than a content library.
