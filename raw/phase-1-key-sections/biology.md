## The Foundation: Parental Investment Theory

**Robert Trivers (1972)** is where everything starts. The core logic: whichever sex bears higher obligatory reproductive cost (gestation, lactation) will be *more selective* in mate choice, while the lower-investing sex will compete *intra-sexually* for access. In humans, female obligatory investment (pregnancy, childbirth) is structurally higher than male (sperm), which is why women trend toward greater mate selectivity and men trend toward greater mate competition. This is the **root** — every other theory below is downstream of this. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parental_investment)

***

## The Primary Framework: Sexual Strategies Theory

**Buss & Schmitt (1993)** built on Trivers to argue that both men and women have evolved a *repertoire* of strategies — not one fixed behavior — sliding along a **short-term to long-term continuum** depending on context: [psychology.iresearchnet](https://psychology.iresearchnet.com/social-psychology/social-psychology-theories/sexual-strategies-theory/)

- **Men in short-term contexts** prioritize sexual access and minimize commitment
- **Men in long-term contexts** prioritize fertility cues (youth, health, physical attractiveness)
- **Women in short-term contexts** seek genetic quality (physical masculinity, dominance cues)
- **Women in long-term contexts** prioritize resource acquisition ability, status, and commitment [psychology.iresearchnet](https://psychology.iresearchnet.com/social-psychology/social-psychology-theories/sexual-strategies-theory/)

The critical nuance the Red Pill removes: these are **context-activated strategies**, not fixed personality types. A man isn't "wired to cheat" — he has a short-term strategy that gets activated under specific conditions. Same for women. [pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8483982/)

## The Contested Layer: Dual Mating Strategy

This is where the science gets genuinely murky. The **Dual Mating Hypothesis** (also called **Strategic Pluralism**) proposes that women simultaneously optimize for two things: [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategic_pluralism)

- A **"good genes" partner** — masculine, dominant, genetically fit (short-term signal)
- A **"good provider" partner** — stable, invested, resource-rich (long-term signal)

The **Ovulatory Shift Hypothesis** is an extension: women's preference for masculine/dominant men allegedly *peaks near ovulation* and shifts back toward stable/caring men at other cycle phases. This is the scientific basis for the "women cheat during ovulation" claim in the text you shared. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ovulatory_shift_hypothesis)

**The honest caveat:** two meta-analyses on this produced *directly contradictory results* — one found a small but real effect, one found no support at all. David Buss himself — the architect of Sexual Strategies Theory — has expressed skepticism of the dual mating model, citing cuckoldry rate estimates (once claimed at 13–20%, now revised to 1–2%) that undermine the premise. **This is a genuinely contested area, not settled science.** [utupub](https://www.utupub.fi/bitstream/handle/10024/150453/Soini_Eetu_opinnaytetyo.pdf?sequence=1)

## The Coolidge Effect

The rat novelty study is real and well-replicated in many mammalian species. In humans, evidence exists that *sexual novelty increases arousal* in both sexes, but the effect is considerably stronger and more behaviorally expressed in men — consistent with Parental Investment Theory (lower investment = more to gain from variety). The Red Pill version presents this as "men are programmed to cheat" — the actual finding is that novelty-seeking is a *graduated biological tendency*, not an imperative. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parental_investment)
