# Source summary

This source argues that marriages fail less because couples disagree and more
because they lose friendship, turn against bids, open conflict badly, miss repair
attempts, and interpret each other through a negative filter.

Its strongest MarriageOS value is structural:

- friendship is not sentimental extra credit; it is core infrastructure
- knowing your partner's inner world protects against drift
- admiration protects against contempt
- daily turning-toward behaviour builds trust over time
- harsh start-up, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling, and flooding are
  the practical anatomy of destructive conflict
- many recurring conflicts are not fully solvable and require understanding,
  not total victory
- shared meaning and rituals give a couple a life that feels joined, not merely managed

For MarriageOS, this is less a “tips” source than a system source. It supplies
clean explanatory logic for why a bond weakens, why certain fights repeat,
and what small relational moves are disproportionately powerful.

# Editor notes

Use confidently, but translate hard.

Best fit:
- quiet-drift repair
- we-keep-fighting repair
- gridlock repair
- knowledge on anti-contempt, bids, and shared meaning
- productised script packs and checklists

Do not import directly:
- predictive-stat or questionnaire tone as if users should diagnose themselves by numbers
- dated male/female generalisations as doctrine
- laboratory authority voice as if evidence alone changes behaviour
- workbook bulk and long quiz structures
