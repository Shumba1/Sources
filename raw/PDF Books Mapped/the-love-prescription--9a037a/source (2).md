# Source summary

This source argues that strong relationships are built less by grand gestures
than by repeated micro-actions. Its core thesis is "small things often":
turning toward bids, asking open questions, showing appreciation, making
admiration visible, asking directly for needs, increasing affectionate touch,
and protecting date time.

For MarriageOS, the strongest value is not the seven-day challenge wrapper.
The strongest value is the mechanism layer underneath it:
- parallel lives produce loneliness
- missed bids starve connection
- unspoken needs harden into criticism and resentment
- affection outside sex keeps desire alive
- admiration and gratitude protect against contempt
- date rituals restore vitality, curiosity, and play

# Editor notes

Use selectively but confidently.

Best fit:
- quiet-drift repair
- resentment-building repair
- desire/familiarity knowledge
- companionship and anti-roommate logic
- practical micro-scripts and reset routines

Do not import directly:
- upbeat "one week fixes everything" tone
- over-bright self-help packaging
- simplified metric language as if all couples can self-diagnose from ratios
- light suggestions as if they are enough for coercion, contempt, affairs, or abuse
