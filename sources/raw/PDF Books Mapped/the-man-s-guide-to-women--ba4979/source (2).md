# Source registration

## Core assessment

This source is highly salvageable, but only in pieces.

It should **not** be imported into MarriageOS as a worldview. It is too gender-essentialist,
too one-sided in its framing, and too willing to universalise claims about men and women.

It **should** be mined for specific mechanisms that translate well into MarriageOS:
- attunement beats fixing
- emotional safety is a precondition for intimacy
- conflict escalates when flooding and defensiveness take over
- father involvement and shared family burden matter
- trust is built or eroded through repeated ordinary moments

## Strongest conversion lanes

1. **repair/we-keep-fighting**  
   Best fit. The conflict chapter gives a usable repair spine: flooding, criticism, defensiveness,
   stonewalling, and three grounding questions.

2. **repair/pressure-loop**  
   Strong fit. The source repeatedly argues that desire drops when safety, attunement, and nonsexual
   affection disappear, and that conflict contamination kills erotic momentum.

3. **guides/his-perspective**  
   Strong justified guide fit. The book's main practical value is explaining a common male-side failure
   pattern: solving, dismissing, defending, withdrawing, then wondering why the bond is colder.

## Major quarantine zones

The following should be treated as low-value or unsafe-to-import without heavy rewriting:
- dating-market performance advice
- shopping and appearance stereotyping
- body-image and attractiveness commentary that reinforces gender pressure
- universalised claims about hormones and female psychology
- explicit sexual scripts that flatten consent nuance or presume heterosexual male access logic
