# Source summary

*Passionate Marriage* is a clinically influential intimacy-and-desire source built
around one big claim: many long-term relationship problems are not caused by lack of
love alone, but by emotional fusion, weak self-soothing, avoidance of being seen, and
the collapse of erotic contact into routine or pressure.

For MarriageOS, its biggest value is not the advanced sexual experimentation. Its
biggest value is the mechanism layer beneath it:

- people often flatten desire because wanting feels risky
- routine sex often becomes technique without contact
- couples stay gridlocked because they dodge real choices
- pressure loops are often anxiety loops
- selfhood and closeness grow together, not apart
- stable relationships can become stale when comfort is worshipped above growth

# Editor notes

Best fit:
- repair work where desire has gone flat or touch feels loaded
- repair work where couples are stuck in repetitive gridlock or passive aggression
- knowledge content explaining desire, familiarity, fusion, and mature eroticism

Use with caution:
- explicit sexual language and advanced practices
- therapy-jargon density
- framing that could sound like a new sexual performance standard
- occasional older-style gender assumptions or overconfident clinician voice

Reject as canonical posture:
- any implication that intense sexual openness is the baseline users should already meet
- any use of advanced practices as a new moral test
- any framing that pressures people into sexual escalation instead of safety, consent, and emotional readiness
