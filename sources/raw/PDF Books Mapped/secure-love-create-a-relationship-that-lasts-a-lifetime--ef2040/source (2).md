# Source summary

This source argues that most couple pain is not best understood as surface
content but as insecure attachment showing up through repeated negative
communication cycles. It gives MarriageOS a structured way to translate
common relationship distress into a sharper sequence:

1. identify the unmet attachment need
2. identify the protective stance each partner takes
3. name the negative cycle as the real enemy
4. interrupt the cycle
5. repair the rupture
6. rebuild an attachment-friendly environment over time

The strongest material is not the attachment theory history. The strongest
material is the operational layer:
- “the problem beneath the problem” framing
- felt-sense attachment needs
- anxious / avoidant / disorganized adaptation logic
- negative cycle anatomy
- interruption and repair steps
- emotional validation and safety
- sex as a bond-sensitive system rather than a standalone appetite issue

# Editor notes

Use assertively, but do not import this source wholesale.

Best fit:
- repeated fight loops
- protest / withdraw dynamics
- chronic coexistence and quiet disconnect
- attachment-driven sexual pursue / withdraw
- repair language and rupture-healing logic
- self / partner reframe away from enemy logic

Use with caution:
- attachment-style labels can become identity cages
- many readers will over-pathologise themselves or their partner
- do not imply attachment explains abuse, coercion, or all incompatibility
- keep MarriageOS language cleaner and less therapy-room-heavy than the source
