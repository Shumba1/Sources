# Source summary

This chapter argues that early romantic intensity can make partners see each other, and present themselves to each other, in idealised ways. Its most usable MarriageOS contribution is not the chapter’s evolutionary worldview, but the pattern it keeps surfacing: the early bond can create overly confident expectations about desire, commitment, attention, and sexual rhythm.

The source is especially relevant where a couple now feels trapped inside a "changed deal" experience. One partner may still feel committed while wanting less sex or less intensity; the other may read that change as rejection, withdrawal, or a broken promise. That bilateral misreading is the part worth carrying forward into MarriageOS.
