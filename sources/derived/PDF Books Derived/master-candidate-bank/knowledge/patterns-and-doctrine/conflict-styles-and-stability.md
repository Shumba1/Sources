# Conflict Styles and Stability

There is no single healthy way for couples to disagree.

That matters, because a lot of people assume:
- high conflict always means instability
- low conflict always means health

Both are wrong.

## The useful distinction

The more useful distinction is not style alone. It is regulation versus dysregulation.

### More regulated relationships
These couples may look very different from one another:
- some are calm and lower-conflict
- some are more emotionally expressive
- some avoid certain hot topics but remain connected and non-lonely

### More dysregulated relationships
These couples are more likely to show:
- repeated negative reciprocity
- failed repair
- criticism, contempt, defensiveness, or shutdown
- growing distance and isolation

## Why this matters

Two couples can both argue a lot, but only one may be corroding.

Two couples can both argue very little, but only one may be quietly disconnecting.

The real question is:
- can they stay connected while dealing with difference?
- can they repair?
- does the relationship stay warm, shared, and non-lonely?

That is a better stability test than conflict count alone.
