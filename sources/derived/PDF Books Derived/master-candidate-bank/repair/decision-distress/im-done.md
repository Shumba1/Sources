# I’m Done

When you feel done, the goal is not to force yourself cheerful. The goal is to sort what kind of “done” this is before pain makes the whole future answer itself tonight.

## Best used when…

- You keep thinking, “I cannot do this anymore.”
- You are swinging between leaving, staying, and shutting down.
- You are not sure whether this is unbearable, unsafe, or just deeply depleted.
- You want clarity, but your emotions are louder than your judgment.

## Not for…

- violence, intimidation, coercion, stalking, or real fear
- a home that is objectively unsafe for you or your children
- active abuse being relabelled as “just a rough season”

## What’s Happening

There are at least three different states people confuse when they say they are done:

- **harmful** — this is unsafe, toxic, or dangerous
- **unhappy and activated** — this is painful, flooded, and unstable
- **surviving** — this is numb, flat, and emotionally disconnected

Those states are not the same.

Part of the problem is that intense pain compresses time. It makes tonight feel like the whole truth. It can turn a real feeling into a full verdict before you have sorted what kind of problem you are actually in.

You may be done with this version of the marriage.  
That is not yet the same as having to decide the entire future immediately.

## What Makes It Worse

These moves usually make the state more distorted:

- making irreversible decisions while highly activated
- confusing temporary relief with real clarity
- talking only to people who already agree with your most hurt version
- calling the relationship hopeless before you have sorted unsafe from unbearable
- staying in dead survival mode and pretending that counts as repair
- letting one fight carry the weight of the entire marriage story

## Do This First

**Sort for safety first. Slow the verdict second.**

Ask:

1. Is this actually harmful or unsafe?
2. Or is it deeply unhappy, flooded, and unstable?
3. Or have we been surviving for so long that flatness now feels like final truth?

If there is real harm, step out of repair mode and into safety mode.

If there is not real harm, your first move is not to decide everything.  
Your first move is to stop the panic from running the whole system.

## Say This

> I may be done with this version of us, but I do not have to decide the whole future tonight.

Or:

> I need to slow this down enough to tell the difference between unsafe, unbearable, and exhausted.

If you are saying it to your spouse:

> I am not pretending things are fine. I am saying I do not want pain to make the entire decision for me in one surge.

## Do This in the Next 72 Hours

### 1. Do not make the whole-future decision tonight
Pause any irreversible move that is being driven mainly by emotional flooding, unless safety requires action now.

### 2. Classify the state honestly
Write down which of these is closest:
- harmful
- unhappy and activated
- surviving and numb

### 3. Tell one wise outside person the full truth
Not the edited version. Not the flattering version. The full version.

### 4. Name one stabilising next step
Examples:
- book a therapist or mediator
- sleep separately for a short period without calling it the verdict
- take one structured pause from escalation
- ask for a reality check from someone emotionally mature

### 5. Refuse fantasy exits
Leaving one pain can create others. Staying without repair creates others too. Do not idealise either path.

## 72-Hour Reset

### Day 1
No irreversible decision in peak activation unless safety requires it.

### Day 2
Write down what is actually happening:
- what is hurting
- what feels dangerous
- what is merely intolerable right now
- what has been flat for a long time

### Day 3
Bring in one outside perspective and decide the next small move, not the entire life sentence.

## Escalate If…

Move to a stronger support path if:

- there is fear in your body when you imagine going home
- your children are not emotionally or physically safe
- your spouse uses repair talk to keep you in danger
- the relationship is so shut down that no honest next step can be taken without outside structure
