# Our Conflict Is Hitting the Kids

Children do not need a perfect home. They do need protection from adult tension they were never meant to carry.

## Best used when…

- The kids are seeing too much, hearing too much, or reacting to the climate at home.
- You and your spouse are tense, disrespectful, or openly hostile in front of them.
- One or both of you are venting to the children about the other parent.
- You suspect the house atmosphere is becoming emotionally unsafe for them.

## Not for…

- a home with violence, terror, or real danger
- situations where “protect the kids” is being used to force someone to stay in harm
- any setup where professional safety support is urgently needed

## What’s Happening

Children absorb climate as much as content.

That means they are affected not only by the words you say, but by:

- tension in the room
- coldness
- contempt
- triangulation
- being used as messenger, witness, comfort object, or judge
- hearing one parent degrade the other

Some parents assume separation will solve this automatically. Sometimes it reduces exposure. Sometimes it does not. If the unresolved contempt and bad communication stay alive, children still carry the fallout.

## What Makes It Worse

These moves usually make the child’s environment less secure:

- arguing in front of them and acting like they are too young to notice
- asking them to take sides
- venting to them about the other parent
- speaking disrespectfully about the other parent in their hearing
- using them as emotional support
- pretending they are unaffected because they are quiet

## Do This First

**Take the child out of the conflict system immediately.**

That means:

- no adult venting to them
- no message-carrying
- no side-taking
- no “tell your dad / tell your mum”
- no treating them like a witness who confirms your version

Your child needs to stay in the child role.

## Say This

To yourself:

> My child is not my confidant, ally, witness, or regulator.

To your spouse:

> Whatever happens between us, I do not want the kids carrying our conflict.

To your child, if they have seen too much:

> You may have seen us speak badly or act tense. That was adult behaviour, not your job to manage. We are sorry you were put in the middle of that.

## Do This Tonight

### 1. Have one adult-only conversation
Away from the kids. No performance. No audience.

### 2. Agree on three immediate child-protection rules
Examples:
- we do not vent to the kids
- we do not insult each other in front of them
- we do not recruit them into adult tension

### 3. Repair with the child if needed
If they have clearly seen or heard too much, give a simple age-appropriate apology and reassurance.

### 4. Show one visible respect cue
Not for appearances. For atmosphere.

## 7-Day Reset

### Day 1
No trashing the other parent in front of the kids.

### Day 2
Remove the child from any messenger role.

### Day 3
If you slip, repair it directly and simply.

### Day 4
Name one strength of the other parent aloud in front of the child, if truthful and appropriate.

### Day 5
Keep one hard adult conversation entirely out of earshot.

### Day 6
Notice what changes in the child’s behaviour when the atmosphere is calmer.

### Day 7
Review the week and keep the three rules that helped most.

## Escalate If…

Move to a stronger support path if:

- your child is frightened by the home environment
- one parent is frightening, violent, or unsafe
- you cannot stop triangulating the child
- the child is showing obvious distress and the adult system remains chaotic
