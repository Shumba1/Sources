## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the same fight keeps coming back, and both people feel like the other is the problem.
- likely user state/substate: “we fight about everything”; “nothing gets resolved”; “I feel attacked and shut down”; “I feel like I am always the reasonable one.”
- what is happening: the conflict is running through blame language, hidden expectations, escalation physiology, and distorted victim stories.
- what makes it worse: “you always / you never”; trying to win; jumping to logic too fast; making the partner’s weakness the whole story; staying in the red zone.
- first corrective principle: lower threat before solving content.
- first behaviour move: stop the fight before red-zone damage and replace accusation with description and validation.
- script logic: support language like “we are not fighting only about the thing; we are fighting through old stories and assumptions.”
- tonight move: one short de-escalated conversation that names the pattern, not the whole marriage verdict.
- reset logic: one week of interrupting blame words, noticing zones, validating once before fixing, and asking one curiosity question before arguing a case.
- escalation boundary: if contempt, fear, coercion, or humiliation dominate, this module is no longer enough.

## 6. Claims to preserve

- blame-and-shame destroys safety
