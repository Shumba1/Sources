## 2. Target fit summary

This source is extremely strong on child-impact dynamics.  
Its main value is not “stay together for the kids.” Its main value is “your kids are absorbing the climate, regardless of whether you stay or split.”  
The final module should therefore focus on atmosphere, respect, triangulation, apology, and safety.  
It must not shame adults into staying in harmful relationships.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: children are seeing, hearing, or carrying too much adult conflict.
- likely user state/substate: “the kids are picking up everything”; “we are putting them in the middle”; “the house feels tense all the time.”
- what is happening: the child is adapting to the climate, not just the arguments.
- what makes it worse: disrespect in front of kids, venting to them, recruiting them, letting them carry impressions of one parent as bad or unsafe when that is not the actual issue, pretending they are unaffected.
- first corrective principle: protect the child from the climate before you solve the whole marriage.
- first behaviour move: stop using the child as witness, confidant, messenger, or verdict-collector.
- script logic: support parental apology and a reset of the household tone.
- tonight move: one repair conversation between adults away from children, then one direct age-appropriate reassurance to the child if they have seen too much.
- reset logic: one week of no trashing the other parent in front of children, no emotional leaning on them, and one visible respect cue daily.
- escalation boundary: if the child is in a violent, frightening, or objectively harmful environment, safety planning outranks atmosphere repair.

## 6. Claims to preserve

- children absorb climate as much as content
- splitting does not automatically remove conflict effects
- do not recruit or vent to children
- respect for the other parent in front of children matters
- apology can stabilise a child after exposure to conflict
