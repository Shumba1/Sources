# Daily Connection Ritual

## One-sentence interpretation
You are likely in a repeat cycle where misread intent is driving distance faster than either of you can name it.

## Best used when…
- The same argument keeps replaying.
- You both still have goodwill but feel stuck.

## Not for…
- Active abuse, coercion, surveillance, or relationships without goodwill.

## What’s Happening
A predictable trigger-response loop is compressing emotional safety.

## What Makes It Worse
- Mind-reading the partner's motive.
- Bringing in old scores during new conflict.
- Waiting for perfect words before taking the first repair step.

## Do This First
Pause the content fight and name the cycle in one line.

## Say This
“I think we are in our loop again. I want to reset the tone first, then solve one thing.”

## Do This Tonight
Spend 15 minutes on one concrete reset action (shared task, short walk, or one appreciation each).

## 7-Day Reset
1. Day 1: define the loop in neutral language.
2. Day 2: agree one de-escalation phrase.
3. Day 3: remove one repeat trigger from logistics.
4. Day 4: run a 10-minute repair check-in.
5. Day 5: do one low-pressure connection ritual.
6. Day 6: review what improved and what regressed.
7. Day 7: choose next week's single stabiliser.

## Escalate If…
- Contempt, intimidation, or chronic shutdown continues.
- Repeated attempts produce no reduction in harm.

## Related next steps
- Open repair/pressure-loop.
- Save one script to re-use in live conflict.
