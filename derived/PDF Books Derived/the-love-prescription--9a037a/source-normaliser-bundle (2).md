- **title:** *The Love Prescription*
- **source_type:** book-pdf
- **overall recommendation:** selective-use, high-value feeder
- **why it matters to MarriageOS:**  
  Strong because it translates relationship maintenance into low-friction daily actions. It is unusually good at turning vague repair advice into observable behaviour: notice bids, ask one real question, thank your partner, compliment them, ask clearly for what you need, touch more, protect date time.
- **main weakness:**  
  The book is upbeat, brief, and strongly skewed toward low-to-moderate distress couples. It must not be allowed to overrun MarriageOS doctrine when the real problem is contempt, coercion, active betrayal, or threat.

## 4. Keep / ignore / caution

### Keep
- small things often
- bids for connection as major predictor
- emotional bank account logic
- friendship before conflict-work
- open-ended questions and updated love maps
- routine gratitude and admiration
- direct need expression without criticism
- softened startup
- nonsexual touch as real bond medicine
- date night as anti-devitalization, not luxury

### Ignore
- overly cheery "seven easy days" wrapper
- glossy self-help uplift as if effortlessness is the point
- breezy miracle language
- workshop-marketing energy
- overreliance on named researchers as rhetorical authority in final user copy

### Caution
- headline metrics like 20:1 and 5:1 should be used directionally, not as self-scoring doctrine
- research anecdotes should be translated into practical meaning rather than treated as deterministic
- the book underweights severe cases; do not let its tone flatten danger signals
- "date night" can sound juvenile, pressured, or unrealistic if not translated into protected connection time
- touch guidance must remain consent- and safety-sensitive

## 5. Conflict check against MarriageOS

### Conflict item 1
- **source idea:** A seven-day challenge can create major relational change quickly.
- **point of tension:** MarriageOS cannot promise speed as if all distress is shallow.
- **action:** translate
- **reason:** Keep the micro-action logic, not the implied timeline guarantee.

### Conflict item 2
- **source idea:** Conflict should often be de-centred in favour of friendship work.
- **point of tension:** True for many couples, but dangerous if acute betrayal, coercion, or contempt is present.
- **action:** qualify
- **reason:** Keep the sequence for low-to-moderate distress; branch earlier for severe cases.

### Conflict item 3
- **source idea:** Touch and dating are broadly restorative.
- **point of tension:** Can misfire if pressure, fear, trauma, or safety concerns are active.
- **action:** qualify
- **reason:** Use only with explicit non-demand and safety framing.

### Conflict item 4
- **source idea:** Gratitude and admiration counter contempt.
- **point of tension:** True directionally, but should not be used to excuse real structural neglect or exploitation.
- **action:** translate
- **reason:** Preserve the mechanism without erasing legitimate grievance.

## 7. Final verdict

This source is a **strong feeder for early-stage repair**. It should not be
used as doctrine for high-threat marriages, but it is very good at helping a
couple stop bleeding connection through neglect, missed bids, criticism,
and endless logistics.

Its strongest contributions are:
- anti-roommate logic
- bid-awareness
- appreciation and admiration routines
- direct need-expression
- affectionate touch as bond medicine
- date/play/curiosity as vitality inputs

It should influence:
- repair modules on drift and resentment
- knowledge explaining desire loss under familiarity and logistics
- script products for asking cleanly for needs

It should not control:
- severe-case safety decisions
- top-level doctrine about betrayal, coercion, or contempt
- any implied promise that "small habits" are sufficient in acute danger cases
