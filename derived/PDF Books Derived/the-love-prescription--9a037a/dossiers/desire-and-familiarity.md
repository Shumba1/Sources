## 5. Core content logic

- core thesis: desire often weakens when the relationship becomes operational, overfamiliar, and touch-starved.
- user problem this explains: “We love each other, so why does sex feel absent, flat, or hard to start?”
- what makes desire fade: parallel lives, exhaustion, no affection apart from sex, no play, low curiosity, little couple time.
- what helps desire return: touch that is not automatically loaded, visible couple priority, fun, adventure, questions that reopen the inner world, affectionate rituals.
- what this article must not imply: that every desire problem is solved by cuddling or date night.
- branch conditions: coercion, contempt, trauma activation, painful sex, fear, betrayal, or explicit libido mismatch requiring deeper work.

## 6. Claims to preserve

- sex problems are often larger relationship vitality problems
- touch matters even when it is nonsexual
- date time is about aliveness, not performance
- play and novelty help desire
- parallel lives erode erotic connection
- affection outside the bedroom matters

## 7. Claims to soften or translate

### Item 1
- original idea: more touch automatically means more sex
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: too linear and too pressurising
- translation: low-pressure touch often makes desire easier, safer, and more available

### Item 2
- original idea: date night is the key to passion
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: one ritual cannot carry everything
- translation: protected couple time helps reopen the conditions under which desire can return

## 8. Claims to quarantine or reject

### Item 2
