# Purpose

Help a couple when one person feels the relationship they entered is not the relationship they are now living, especially around desire, initiation, affection, and implicit promises.

# Audience

Couples with remaining goodwill where desire mismatch or changing intensity is being read as rejection, withdrawal, or a changed deal.

Not for:
- active abuse
- coercion around sex or affection
- affair management as a lifestyle
- relationships with no goodwill left

# Output requirements

The final repair module must follow the standard 7-part anatomy:
1. What’s Happening
2. What Makes It Worse
3. Do This First
4. Say This
5. Do This Tonight
6. 7-Day Reset
7. Escalate If…

The module must also include:
- one-sentence interpretation
- Best used when
- Not for

# Required claims

- The early bond can train both partners to expect a level of ease, desire, or initiation that does not automatically sustain itself.
- A drop in desire does not automatically equal a drop in commitment.
- Two people can feel misled by the same change for different reasons.
- Hidden assumptions about what commitment includes become hidden injuries.
- Repair starts by turning silent assumption gaps into explicit adult conversation.

# Required cautions / exclusions

- Do not frame desire drift as deliberate fraud by default.
- Do not moralise libido difference.
- Do not imply entitlement to sex.
- Do not import the chapter’s market-style or grievance-style language.
- Do not overstate gender claims as universal truth.

# Tone rules

- calm
- practical
- emotionally adult
- structured
- non-moralising
- clear under stress

# Canonical constraints

- Repair is the centre of V1.
- The module must be symptom-led, not theory-led.
- The module must sound like a premium operator, not a therapist worksheet.
- Keep all guidance compatible with anti-coercion and anti-surveillance doctrine.
