# Purpose

Explain why the early bonding phase is a bad long-run forecast, especially where couples later experience desire drift, changed effort, or disappointment about what commitment was supposed to include.

# Audience

Couples with remaining goodwill who need a clearer map of why the early phase felt easy and why later familiarity can expose mismatched assumptions.

Not for:
- abstract theory readers with no practical next step
- users looking for blame language about men or women
- users wanting ideology dressed up as science

# Output requirements

The final knowledge article must:
- explain one idea clearly
- stay practical rather than academic
- avoid source-bound worldview claims
- end in a concrete action branch to a repair module

Recommended article anatomy:
1. Core idea
2. Why the early phase misleads
3. What familiarity changes
4. Where the injury starts
5. What to watch for
6. Use this now

# Required claims

- Early attraction often includes idealisation and temporary behavioural boosts.
- The early phase makes certain traits easier to overlook or interpret generously.
- Familiarity exposes the actual pattern and can collapse optimistic assumptions.
- Desire, effort, and commitment do not always move together at the same speed.
- Trouble increases when the couple never updates what they think the bond includes.

# Required cautions / exclusions

- Do not make sweeping claims about immutable male and female nature.
- Do not reproduce the chapter’s “bait and switch” or market-style language.
- Do not turn the article into a sex-frequency debate.
- Do not let the article sit without a practical branch into repair.
