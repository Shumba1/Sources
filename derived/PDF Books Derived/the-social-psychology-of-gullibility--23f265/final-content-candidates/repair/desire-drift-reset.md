A lot of couples are not only fighting about sex or affection. They are reacting to the shock of realising the early version of the relationship trained both of them to expect a pattern that no longer feels shared.

**Best used when**
- one of you feels the relationship changed after commitment
- one of you feels pressured and the other feels shut out
- you still have goodwill, but private scorekeeping has started

**Not for**
- coercion or pressure around sex
- active abuse
- relationships with no goodwill left
- using this module to prove one partner is the problem

## What’s Happening

Early on, a relationship can feel unusually easy, warm, and mutual. That often creates a silent forecast: *this is what us looks like*.

Later, real life, familiarity, stress, children, or simple settling can change the pattern. Desire may change. Initiation may change. Affection may feel less automatic. One partner may still feel committed while the other feels as if something important was quietly removed.

That is why this stage can feel so personal. You are not only dealing with lower desire or less ease. You are dealing with a sense that the relationship contract became different without being re-written out loud.

## What Makes It Worse

- treating desire drift like proof of fraud or bad character
- arguing about who is normal instead of naming the mismatch
- keeping a private scorecard instead of making the assumptions visible
- turning every touch, kiss, or quiet moment into a negotiation
- using pressure, begging, testing, or cold withdrawal
- acting as if commitment, fidelity, affection, and sexual availability all mean the same thing

## Do This First

Run an **expectation reset**, not a blame session.

For one conversation, stop trying to prove who changed more. Instead, each of you answers three questions:

1. What felt easy or natural in the early phase?
2. What did I assume that meant about our long-term relationship?
3. What feels different now, and what story have I been telling myself about that change?

The goal is not to agree immediately. The goal is to surface the hidden contract each of you has been carrying.

## Say This

> I do not want this to become a courtroom conversation.
> I think we are both reacting to a change in the relationship, and we may be reading that change differently.
> I want to understand what each of us thought commitment included, what feels different now, and what we need to rebuild on purpose instead of assuming it should still happen automatically.

## Do This Tonight

Take 20 minutes and make two short lists.

**List A: What I thought would continue**
- affection
- initiation
- sexual rhythm
- emotional availability
- attention
- non-sex closeness

**List B: What still counts as love and commitment to me now**
- practical care
- fidelity
- affection
- responsiveness
- honesty
- effort to repair

Then compare lists without arguing. Just circle:
- where you still match
- where you partly match
- where you assumed the same thing but clearly did not

Finish by agreeing on **one small pattern** to reset this week. Do not try to solve everything in one sitting.

## 7-Day Reset

**Day 1:** Repeat the expectation audit and each name one hidden assumption you had never said out loud.  
**Day 2:** Agree what counts as affection that does not automatically create pressure.  
**Day 3:** Agree one rule for how initiation will happen this week so nobody has to guess.  
**Day 4:** Each name one thing that makes closeness easier and one thing that shuts it down.  
**Day 5:** Re-state what fidelity and commitment mean in practice, not just in theory.  
**Day 6:** Do one non-sex act of warmth on purpose.  
**Day 7:** Decide whether the pressure is reducing. If it is not, choose the next repair conversation instead of drifting back into scorekeeping.

## Escalate If…

- either partner uses pressure, ridicule, or punishment
- the conversation cannot happen without contempt
- secrecy, outsourcing, or side-channel coping is replacing honest discussion
- one of you refuses any reality-based conversation about the changed pattern
- there is not enough goodwill left for this to stay a repair conversation
