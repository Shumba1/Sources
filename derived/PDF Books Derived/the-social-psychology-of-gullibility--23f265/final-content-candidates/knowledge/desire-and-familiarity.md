The early phase of a relationship is often a bad long-run forecast. Not because it is fake, but because it can temporarily amplify desire, optimism, generosity, and positive interpretation in ways the settled bond does not sustain by itself.

## Core idea

When a couple first bonds, both people often read each other through an idealising lens. Traits are easier to admire. Friction is easier to smooth over. Warmth and effort feel more spontaneous. That does not mean the bond is false. It means the signal is unusually flattering.

## Why the early phase misleads

Early attraction can do two things at once:

- make a partner look better than an outside observer would rate them
- make both partners behave like improved versions of themselves for a while

That combination is powerful. It helps commitment form. But it also makes quiet assumptions grow fast:

- this level of desire will keep happening
- this ease is just who we are
- this style of attention is now the baseline
- this trait will always feel attractive rather than costly

## What familiarity changes

Familiarity does not automatically kill a bond, but it does expose the actual pattern.

A trait that once felt relaxed can later feel unreliable. A style that once felt exciting can later feel unstructured. Early sexual rhythm can also prove to be a poor forecast of settled life. That is why disappointment in long relationships often feels bigger than the surface issue. The pain is not only about less desire or less novelty. It is about a forecast collapsing.

## Where the injury starts

The real injury usually starts when the couple never updates the private contract.

One partner may still feel committed while wanting less sex, less intensity, or less initiation. The other may experience that exact same shift as withdrawal, rejection, or a broken promise. Both people can feel hurt while telling very different stories about what changed.

## What to watch for

Watch for these patterns:

- *I thought this was just us* language
- shock that a once-attractive trait now feels difficult
- silent scorekeeping about affection, initiation, or effort
- treating lower desire as automatic proof of lower love
- staying attached to the early version of the relationship instead of reading the present pattern honestly

## Use this now

Do not start with accusation. Start with pattern clarification.

Ask:
- What did we assume the early phase meant?
- What changed in reality?
- Which part of the hurt is about desire, and which part is about expectation?
- What needs to be rebuilt on purpose now instead of assumed?

Then open the matching repair: `repair/desire-drift-reset`.
