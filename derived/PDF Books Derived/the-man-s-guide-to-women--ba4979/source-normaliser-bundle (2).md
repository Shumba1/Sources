# Source normaliser bundle
## The Man's Guide to Women

## 1. Source fit summary

This source is useful precisely where it stops trying to explain all women and starts naming
repeatable relationship mechanics.

The strongest reusable layer is its claim that **trust is built through felt safety and attunement**,
not through reassurance slogans. The best pages show how a common male failure pattern
works: listen poorly, solve too fast, get defensive, flood, withdraw, then wonder why the
relationship feels sexless and adversarial.

A second strong layer is the parenting material. The source is much better when it moves out of
dating performance and into fatherhood, shared family load, and the warning not to compete
with the baby for significance.

A third useful layer is the maintenance doctrine near the end: keep dating, keep learning your
partner, appreciate her, protect trust, and support her dreams.

## 2. Theme clusters

### Cluster A — Trust through attunement
- trustworthiness as reliability + emotional safety
- being there emotionally rather than fixing
- turning toward bids and feelings
- nondefensive listening and empathy

**MarriageOS value:** very high

### Cluster B — Conflict mechanics
- women often protest disconnection; men often experience this as attack
- male flooding drives criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and stonewalling
- downregulation matters before problem-solving
- asking what the partner needs / feels / fears is more useful than arguing facts

**MarriageOS value:** very high

### Cluster C — Desire requires safety
- conflict contamination lowers desire
- nonsexual affection and aftercare matter
- feeling safe and wanted outside the bedroom matters
- pressure, dismissal, or coercive energy suppresses arousal

**MarriageOS value:** high

### Cluster D — Parenting and family stability
- do not compete with the baby for significance
- father involvement strongly shapes children and marriage
- shared childcare and domestic effort reduce resentment
- running on empty after children is common and repairable

**MarriageOS value:** high

### Cluster E — Friendship and outside support
- women need social support networks beyond the couple
- do not isolate a partner from friends
- friendship quality affects health and relationship resilience

**MarriageOS value:** medium-high

### Cluster F — Drift-maintenance reminders
- kiss goodbye and hello
- keep dating
- stay curious
- show appreciation
- honor dreams

**MarriageOS value:** medium-high, but partially duplicated by Love Prescription and Eight Dates

### Cluster G — Quarantine / low-fit material
- performance-oriented dating cues
- shopping stereotypes
- body-essentialist commentary
- alpha/beta or hormonal generalisations
- explicit sexual technique written from a male-access script

**MarriageOS value:** low to negative

## 4. Keep / ignore / caution decisions

### Keep
- attunement
- nondefensive listening
- conflict flooding and self-soothing
- trust as repeated turning-toward
- conflict contamination of desire
- aftercare and nonsexual affection
- father involvement and shared family burden
- anti-isolation stance toward outside support
- curiosity, appreciation, and honoring dreams

### Caution
- strong sex-difference claims
- hormonal and cycle explanations
- fear/safety claims that can turn paternalistic
- statistics presented as universal laws
- explicitly sexual technique sections

### Ignore / quarantine
- status/approach/dating-market tactics
- shopping chapter as gender explanation
- body-essentialist beauty commentary
- "hero/zero" rhetoric
- overtly heteronormative or conquest-coded framing
