## 2. Target fit summary

This source is strong for pressure-loop when stripped of its explicit technique and gender packaging.

The durable insight is not “how to perform.” It is that many partners stop wanting sex when they feel emotionally unsafe, dismissed, overpressured, or only approached erotically after a day of disconnection. Nonsexual touch, safety, anticipation, and aftercare all matter.

This dossier should therefore be built around intimacy preconditions, not seduction tricks. It should not become a sex manual, a male-access guide, or a diagnostic statement about female desire.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: One or both partners are trying to create intimacy after hours or days of emotional distance, unresolved hurt, overload, or pressure. The body does not cooperate because the bond does not feel safe enough yet.
- likely user state/substate: “It feels mechanical”; “We only touch when it’s heading somewhere”; “I can’t just switch on”; “I feel rejected and then push harder.”
- what is happening: emotional safety and desire have uncoupled from the relationship. Touch is no longer neutral. It is loaded.
- what makes it worse: pressuring for access; treating a no as rejection; dismissing emotions earlier in the day; jumping straight to erotic contact without warmth; ignoring exhaustion or life-phase strain; skipping aftercare.
- first corrective principle: connection before conversion.
- first behaviour move: reintroduce agenda-free affection and explicit safety around touch.
- script logic: “I don’t want closeness to feel loaded. Let’s make touch safe and warm again without needing it to turn into something every time.”
- tonight move: 15 to 20 minutes of nonsexual closeness, explicit appreciation, no escalation pressure, and clean ending.
- reset logic: one week of affectionate contact, emotional attunement, and no retaliatory coldness after a no.
- escalation boundary: if there is coercion, intimidation, humiliation, or sexual bargaining, this is no longer a light repair target.
- related guide(s): guides/his-perspective; guides/her-perspective
- related knowledge: knowledge/desire-and-familiarity
