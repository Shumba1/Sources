# Quiet Drift

A marriage does not have to explode to start breaking down.

Sometimes it cools, separates, and thins out.

## Best used when…
- you function together but feel emotionally alone
- the relationship feels more parallel than shared
- talking things through has started to feel pointless
- one or both of you mostly handle life alone now

## What’s happening

Repeated unresolved conflict can turn into distance.

That often follows a pattern:
1. problems feel severe
2. discussion feels useless
3. each person starts solving alone
4. the marriage becomes more parallel
5. loneliness grows inside the relationship

This is not peace. It is disconnection with daily function still working.

## Signs you may be here
- fewer real check-ins
- low fondness in how you speak about the relationship
- more separateness than we-ness
- private coping replacing shared coping
- a subtle sense of “we live beside each other now”

## Do this first

Do not treat this as just a mood.

Name it clearly:
> I do not think we are only busy. I think we are becoming separate in a way that worries me.

Then move toward one small shared action instead of one more abstract discussion.
