# We Keep Fighting

Some couples do not have a disagreement problem. They have an escalation problem.

## Best used when…
- arguments turn sharp fast
- one criticism becomes a chain reaction
- one or both of you ends up flooded, defensive, contemptuous, or shut down
- repair attempts keep getting missed

## What’s happening

The danger is not conflict by itself. The danger is a loop where negativity keeps breeding more negativity.

That usually looks like:
- criticism instead of a specific complaint
- contempt instead of respect
- defensiveness instead of ownership
- shutdown instead of steady engagement

Once that loop starts, it gets harder to hear anything good in what the other person is trying to do. Even repair attempts can land as one more jab.

## What makes it worse
- arguing while flooded
- talking as if your partner’s flaw is the whole problem
- trying to win instead of slow the exchange
- treating shutdown as proof of not caring rather than a sign the system is overheating

## Do this first

Name the loop, not just the issue.

Try:
> We are not solving this. We are escalating it.  
> I think we need to get out of the loop before we keep hurting the bond.

## Immediate repair rule
If one of you is flooded:
- slow down
- lower intensity
- do not chase with more criticism
- do not weaponise the other person’s shutdown
- come back when the system is calmer

## Watch for these markers
- global blame
- mockery or eye-rolling
- instant self-protection
- emotional disappearance

When those show up, you are no longer just discussing the issue.
