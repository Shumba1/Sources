## Objective
Explain that stable marriages are not all the same and that instability is more about dysregulation, corrosive patterns, and loneliness than about one single style.

## Included extract rationale
- `EXT-WPD-006`: stable styles distinction
- `EXT-WPD-008`: conflict avoidance nuance
- `EXT-WPD-001`: negative reciprocity as instability marker
- `EXT-WPD-003`: four horsemen as high-risk corrosion pattern

## Core content logic
- Conflict frequency alone is a weak guide.
- Some couples are intense but regulated.
- Some couples are low-conflict but still connected.
- The dangerous zone is when the system is nonregulated: repeated escalation, failed repair, shutdown, and growing isolation.

## Preserve
- regulated vs nonregulated logic
- conflict-style diversity
- instability markers over style stereotypes

## Soften / translate
- typology jargon
- 'adaptive probabilism' and other technical terms

## Reject
- one-size-fits-all claims about what a healthy marriage must look like
