# What Predicts Divorce? — Source Normaliser Bundle

## Source registration summary

This is a landmark Gottman research text. It is more useful to MarriageOS as a
mechanism source than as a tips source. The strongest transferable layers are:
negative reciprocity, flooding, the criticism/contempt/defensiveness/stonewalling
cascade, distance-and-isolation dynamics, and differentiated conflict styles.

## Theme clusters

### CL-WPD-01 — Escalation mechanics
- negative reciprocity
- harsh start-up equivalents
- criticism -> contempt -> defensiveness -> stonewalling

### CL-WPD-02 — Flooding and shutdown
- diffuse physiological arousal / flooding
- inability to hear repair
- male withdrawal / stonewalling patterns (translate carefully)

### CL-WPD-03 — Distance and isolation
- severe-problems framing
- solve-it-alone stance
- parallel lives
- loneliness within the marriage

### CL-WPD-04 — Stable conflict styles
- not all low-conflict marriages are dysfunctional
- regulated vs nonregulated systems
- validating / volatile / conflict-avoiding stability logic

### CL-WPD-05 — Relationship narrative as signal
- oral-history interview
- fondness versus disappointment
- we-ness versus separateness
- chaos / disillusionment framing

## Keep / ignore / caution

### Keep
- negative reciprocity as a more useful signal than raw negativity alone
- repair failure under flooding
- four horsemen as a cascade, not isolated bad habits
- emotional distance as a later-stage process, not just a mood
- stable marriages can look different from one another

### Ignore
- highly technical coding-system detail unless needed for doctrine
- older epidemiological scene-setting
- dense methods exposition that does not affect downstream content

### Caution
- historic claims about men/women should be translated into pattern language
- percentages and prediction claims should not become sales copy
- avoid over-clinical language in final candidate content

## Final verdict

This is a major doctrine source for MarriageOS. It should shape process language,
branching logic, and how the system distinguishes escalation, shutdown, and
dead-marriage drift. It is not a strong source for warm scripts on its own. It is a
strong source for structural understanding.
