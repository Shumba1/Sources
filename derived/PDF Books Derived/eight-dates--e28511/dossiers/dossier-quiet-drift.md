## 2. Target fit summary

This target exists to catch couples who are not in a dramatic crisis but are clearly flattening into roommate mode, politeness, logistics, or emotional distance.  
The selected extracts fit because they explain drift as cumulative underfeeding rather than one explosive event: missed prioritisation, conflict avoidance, reduced affection, reduced positivity, and loss of novelty.  
The dossier is strong because it stays symptom-led and repair-led.  
It gives MarriageOS a way to intervene before resentment calcifies.  
It should not become a date-night sermon, a romance essay, or a generic “keep the spark alive” article.  
It also should not become a hidden sex module. The bond comes first, not escalation.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: The couple is not exploding. They are thinning out. Conversation becomes logistical, affection becomes sparse, novelty disappears, and each partner quietly starts feeling less chosen.
- likely user state/substate: “Nothing is terribly wrong, but something is missing”; “We are polite, tired, and flat”; “We do not really fight, but we do not really meet either.”
- what is happening: Drift is operating as cumulative de-prioritisation. Small misses, low curiosity, low affection, and low positivity are starving the bond.
- what makes it worse: conflict avoidance mistaken for harmony; waiting for a bigger romantic fix; treating fun as optional; reading flatness as permanent truth; interpreting the partner only through irritation or absence.
- first corrective principle: restore light contact before heavy conversation. Rebuild warmth and felt priority in small, repeatable ways.
- first behaviour move: introduce one low-pressure priority signal immediately — eye contact, affectionate greeting, short check-in, physical closeness without agenda, or one sincere appreciation.
- script logic: the draft should help the user say “I do not think we are broken. I think we have gone flat. I want to turn back toward you in small ways instead of waiting for a big moment.”
- tonight move: one short, protected reconnection block with no logistics, no screens, and no forced deep processing; add one element of gentle novelty or play.
- reset logic: seven days of small turning-toward behaviour, affection without hidden agenda, visible appreciation, and one shared low-friction change from routine.
- escalation boundary: if the flatness masks contempt, active secrecy, active comparison to outsiders, repeated refusal of any soft reconnection, or fear-based shutdown, the module should branch out rather than overpromise.
- related guide(s): guides/read-both
- related product(s): products/hard-conversation-scripts-pack; optional future novelty-planner product

## 6. Claims to preserve

- Quiet drift is usually cumulative, not dramatic.
- Low conflict does not automatically mean high connection.
- Small priority signals matter more than grand reassurance.
- Affection outside the bedroom helps rebuild the bond.
- Play, laughter, and novelty are maintenance inputs, not indulgences.
- Restoring positive interpretation and fondness is part of repair.

## 7. Claims to soften or translate

### Item 1
- original idea: Weekly date night is the central maintenance ritual.
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: MarriageOS is micro-intervention-first, not event-planning-first.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: short protected reconnection windows, tiny rituals, and repeatable turning-toward behaviour.

### Item 2
- original idea: Relationship success depends on specific positivity ratios.
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: Scorekeeping language is brittle and can feel mechanical.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: the relationship needs noticeably more warmth, fondness, and good-faith interpretation than irritation and dismissal.

- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: The product cannot imply that repair requires elaborate experiences or extroverted energy.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: add some newness, surprise, or lightness appropriate to the couple’s actual bandwidth.
