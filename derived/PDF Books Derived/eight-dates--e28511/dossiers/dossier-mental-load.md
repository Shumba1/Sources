## 2. Target fit summary

This target exists to address a major V1 repair surface: the feeling that one person is carrying the system while the other sees only fragments of the load.  
The selected extracts fit because they cover the three layers MarriageOS needs for this problem: invisible unpaid labour, work acting as a third party, and money conflict reflecting meaning rather than mere arithmetic.  
The dossier is strong because it turns mental load into a partnership-structure issue rather than a character defect accusation.  
It should not become a budgeting guide, a feminist theory article, or a generic “share chores better” page.  
It must stay fast, practical, and emotionally adult.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: One partner is carrying planning, remembering, tracking, arranging, or emotional coordination work that the other partner does not fully see. Paid work strain may also be swallowing the bond.
- likely user state/substate: “I cannot relax because I am holding the whole system in my head”; “Nothing gets done unless I track it”; “I work hard too, but it never seems to count”; “Every money conversation becomes moral.”
- what is happening: The couple is fighting about tasks, time, or spending, but the deeper issue is unseen load, symbolic meaning, and bond starvation under work pressure.
- what makes it worse: comparing tiredness instead of mapping load; reducing the partner to a spender/saver stereotype; measuring contribution only in paid work; letting work absorb all good energy; treating appreciation as unnecessary because the work is expected.
- first corrective principle: make the load visible before you argue about fairness.
- first behaviour move: one short naming pass of recurring invisible work, one acknowledgement of pressure on each side, and one immediate rebalance step that reduces tonight’s burden rather than solving the entire household.
- script logic: the final draft should support language like “I do not think this is just about dishes or spending. I think the system is overloaded and we are not seeing the same work.”
- tonight move: a fast load audit with three columns — visible tasks, invisible tasks, urgent relief — followed by one concrete transfer or shutdown of work.
- reset logic: one week of visible ownership, explicit task handoff, one appreciation per day for paid or unpaid contribution, and one boundary against work swallowing all connection time.
- escalation boundary: if there is contempt for unpaid work, financial secrecy, refusal to participate in basic load-sharing, or chronic work over-identification that leaves no room for the bond, the module must branch outward.

## 6. Claims to preserve

- Mental load is not just chores; it includes remembering, tracking, anticipating, and coordinating.
- Unpaid labour can be a major respect and resentment issue.
- Money fights often hide deeper fears and meanings.
- Work can behave like a third party in the relationship.
- Appreciation for paid and unpaid contribution matters.
- Repair starts with visibility and relief, not moral accusation.
