## 2. Target fit summary

This target exists to stabilise one of MarriageOS V1’s strongest repair surfaces: affection and intimacy turning into a predictable pressure circuit.  
The selected extracts fit because they give direct support for four core mechanisms: sex talk belongs outside the bedroom, “no” must not trigger punishment, desire needs out-of-bedroom warmth, and life-phase stress radically changes capacity.  
This dossier is especially strong because it avoids grievance framing and instead keeps both partners inside the same repair problem.  
It should not become a libido debate, a gender war, or a sexual-entitlement page.  
It also should not become a broad sex-advice article. The target is a repair module under stress.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: Touch, closeness, or romance has become predictably linked to escalation. One partner braces. The other starts reading distance as rejection.
- likely user state/substate: “Every touch turns into a test”; “I can’t relax because I know where this is heading”; “I feel unwanted and keep trying harder”; “I say no and then feel guilty or punished.”
- what is happening: The bond is being overloaded by anticipatory pressure. Affection is no longer neutral or comforting because it feels like a funnel toward sex or disappointment.
- what makes it worse: sulking after a no; treating reassurance as fake unless it leads to sex; only being affectionate when trying to initiate; discussing sex only in the moment; ignoring exhaustion, postpartum realities, health, body stress, or overload.
- first corrective principle: separate affection from escalation and separate refusal from rejection.
- first behaviour move: establish one immediate rule that non-sexual affection can exist without conversion pressure and that a no must be followed by bond protection, not punishment.
- script logic: the final draft should support language like “I want touch to feel safe again, not loaded. If one of us is not up for sex, we still protect the bond.”
- tonight move: one low-pressure closeness block with explicit non-escalation agreement and explicit appreciation; the point is safety, not conversion.
- reset logic: one week of visible, agenda-free affection; zero retaliatory coldness after a no; one outside-the-bedroom conversation about what feels good, what feels pressured, and what kind of initiation is easiest to receive.
- escalation boundary: if there is coercion, sexual contempt, fear, trauma activation, repeated use of sex as punishment, or complete inability to tolerate a respectful no, the module must branch out and stop pretending this is a light mismatch.
- related guide(s): guides/his-perspective; guides/her-perspective; optional both-track bridge later
- related product(s): products/hard-conversation-scripts-pack

## 6. Claims to preserve

- A no handled badly creates relational injury beyond the moment.
- Affection cannot repair the bond if it is predictably loaded with demand.
- Desire often depends on safety, rest, emotional connection, and life-stage bandwidth.
- Couples do better when they talk about sex outside the bedroom.
- Pressure falls when affection and reassurance are allowed to exist without forced conversion.
- Appreciation and warmth matter even inside intimacy repair.

## 7. Claims to soften or translate

### Item 1
- original idea: Research-based gender generalisations about how men and women think about sex.
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: Too broad, too easy to overstate, and not sufficiently aligned with MarriageOS parallel-track posture.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: different partners often have different prerequisites, pressure sensitivities, and meanings attached to initiation.

### Item 2
- original idea: Kissing, cuddling, and romance statistically improve sex lives.
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: The module should not sound like hack-based optimisation.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: out-of-bedroom warmth lowers vigilance and helps the bond feel safer and more alive.

### Item 3
- original idea: More explicit “sex review” framing.
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: Can sound clinical or performative if written bluntly.
- how it should be translated into MarriageOS language: have one calm, specific conversation about what helps, what pressures, and what kind of contact each person can receive well.
