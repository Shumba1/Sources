# Mood-Aware Repair
*Source-scoped candidate derived from psychology-of-sociability-p4--96834b*

## The idea
Your state changes how you read your partner.

It changes:
- what you notice
- how much you trust
- how certain you feel
- how fair or self-protective you become

That does not mean mood explains everything.  
It does mean timing and pacing matter more than couples usually admit.

## A useful rule
Before deciding what your partner meant, check the state you are using to read them.

## When not to force a major conversation
Pause if either person is:
- emotionally sped up
- unusually triumphant, euphoric, or overconfident
- already suspicious and reading every detail as evidence
- too flooded to stay concrete

A pause is not avoidance if it includes a return time.

Example:
> “I don’t trust my read on this right now. I want to come back in 30 minutes and do this more carefully.”

## What state can distort
When people are in an upbeat, confident state, they can sometimes:
- rely too quickly on first impressions
- trust too fast
- miss important details
- become more self-protective than they realise

When people are in a lower, more serious state, they can sometimes:
- notice details more carefully
- question easy assumptions
- become less gullible
- attend more to fairness

But do not overlearn this.  
A more serious mood is not automatically better. It is just different.

## The practical translation
Use this framework modestly:

- **do not make meaning too fast**
- **do not negotiate while mentally sloppy**
- **do not confuse suspicion with accuracy**
- **do not confuse confidence with clarity**

## A better conflict format when emotions are active

### 1) State the state
> “I’m more activated than I look.”
> “I’m feeling unusually certain, which means I should slow down.”

### 2) Strip the story back to facts
Say what happened before saying what it meant.

- Fact: “You left the room while I was speaking.”
- Story: “You don’t care what I feel.”

Lead with the fact.

### 3) Ask one clarifying question
> “What was happening for you in that moment?”

### 4) Make one modest interpretation
> “I have a guess, but I don’t want to overread it.”

### 5) Return to fairness
> “What would be a fair next step for both of us?”

## Useful phrases
- “I may be reading too much into this.”
- “Let me re-check what I actually know.”
- “I want to be fair before I am certain.”
- “I need a slower conversation, not a bigger one.”

## Bottom line
Many couples only monitor the topic of the conflict.

Fewer monitor the state they are bringing into it.

But often the state is the hidden force shaping the whole exchange.
