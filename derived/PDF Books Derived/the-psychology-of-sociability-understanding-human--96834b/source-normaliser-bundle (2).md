# Source normaliser bundle

## 1) Source posture

The source is a scholarly, chapter-based review volume. It is strongest at:
- naming core constructs clearly
- linking patterns of behaviour to underlying expectations, mental models, and processing biases
- surfacing experimentally observed tendencies rather than step-by-step repair protocols

## 2) MarriageOS posture conversion

Translate source ideas into MarriageOS language as follows:

- **attachment insecurity** -> “protective pattern under stress”
- **attachment anxiety** -> “closeness alarm / protest pattern”
- **attachment avoidance** -> “distance-protection pattern”
- **hyperactivating strategy** -> “pursue harder, cling, control, or escalate”
- **deactivating strategy** -> “shut down, detach, minimise, or self-contain”
- **perceived partner responsiveness** -> “I feel understood, valued, and supported by you”
- **mood effects on cognition** -> “your state is shaping what you notice, trust, and how strongly you react”

## 3) Hard translation constraints

Do not transfer the following into MarriageOS content without qualification:
- diagnostic framing
- gendered universals
- deterministic claims about personality
- broad claims that sadness is better than happiness
- claims that responsiveness solves all relationship problems

## 4) Content opportunities

### Strong fits
1. Repair guidance on restoring responsiveness during conflict
2. Knowledge content on attachment patterns under stress
3. Repair guidance on slowing conversations when mood is distorting interpretation

### Weak fits
- products
- quizzes
- grand frameworks with too much certainty
- any content that implies one conversation fixes entrenched patterns

## 5) Reliability notes

- Chapter 12 is strongest for pattern language and relational strategy differences
- Chapter 13 is strongest for repair-relevant mechanisms around responsiveness, defensiveness, openness, and emotional disclosure
- Chapter 14 is strongest for conversation-timing, fairness, trust, and interpretive bias
- Chapter 14 should be used cautiously: findings are useful, but the translation into marriage guidance must remain modest and state-sensitive
