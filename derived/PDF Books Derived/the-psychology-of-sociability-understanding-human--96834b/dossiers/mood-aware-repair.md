# Dossier: mood-aware-repair

## Proposed MarriageOS target
- **Slug:** mood-aware-repair
- **Type:** repair
- **Status:** strong fit, but use carefully

## Why this target
Chapter 14 offers a useful repair insight: a couple’s state can change what they notice, trust, and how fair or suspicious they become. This is a strong MarriageOS fit if translated carefully and modestly.

## Core source signals
- Mild mood states can alter processing style.
- Positive mood can encourage broader, more top-down, more trusting processing.
- Negative mood can encourage more detail-focused, accommodative, skeptical processing.
- Laboratory findings suggest mood can shift fairness, trust, and deception detection.

## MarriageOS translation
This should not become “sad is better” content. It should become:
- timing guidance
- pacing guidance
- interpretation guidance

Best working proposition:
> Before you decide what your partner meant, check the state you are using to read them.

## Practical uses
- pause major conversations when one or both partners are emotionally sped up
- slow certainty and re-check details when you feel euphoric, triumphant, or prematurely reassured
- avoid treating temporary suspicion as truth
- use lower-intensity, concrete language when either person is flooded

## What to avoid
- glamorising sadness
- advising people to argue while dysregulated
- implying mood alone explains serious relational harm

## Recommended final shape
A compact repair note with:
1. state affects reading
2. when to pause
3. how to return
4. how to speak concretely instead of interpretively

## Cautions
- Findings are drawn from mild mood manipulations and lab tasks.
- The practical translation should remain probabilistic and modest.
