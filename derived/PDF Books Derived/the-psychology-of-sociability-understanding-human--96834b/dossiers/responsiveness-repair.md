# Dossier: responsiveness-repair

## Proposed MarriageOS target
- **Slug:** responsiveness-repair
- **Type:** repair
- **Status:** strongest fit in Part 4

## Why this target
Chapter 13 provides the clearest practical repair lever in the source set: **perceived partner responsiveness**. It links responsiveness to reduced defensiveness, more open-mindedness, and greater emotional openness. This is highly compatible with MarriageOS repair-first positioning.

## Core source signals
- Responsiveness means feeling **understood, valued, and supported**.
- Responsiveness is associated with thriving relationships and better conflict processes.
- When people feel responsive support, self-protective defensiveness eases.
- Responsiveness supports open-mindedness and tolerance for mixed or difficult information.
- Responsiveness increases willingness to disclose emotion.

## MarriageOS translation
This dossier should become a practical repair piece that helps couples restore enough safety for a stuck conversation to move again.

Best working proposition:
> Repair starts when the other person feels accurately received, not merely corrected.

## Recommended practical moves
- reflect before rebutting
- name the feeling before the fix
- show value before problem-solving
- ask what support would feel useful right now
- reduce speed and certainty

## What to avoid
- claiming responsiveness fixes everything
- vague validation language without behavioural specificity
- turning responsiveness into appeasement or agreement

## Recommended final shape
A short repair guide with:
1. a definition of responsiveness
2. signs of unresponsiveness
3. a 15-20 minute reset protocol
4. a few sentence stems
5. one caution about not faking empathy

## Cautions
- Responsiveness is not surrender.
- Understanding is not agreement.
- Support is not mind-reading; it usually requires asking.
