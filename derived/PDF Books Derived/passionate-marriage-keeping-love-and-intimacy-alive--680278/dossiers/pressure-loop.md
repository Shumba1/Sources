## 2. Target fit summary

This source is exceptionally strong for pressure-loop repair because it explains low desire as more than simple libido mismatch.  
Its most useful mechanism is that a partner may stop wanting not because nothing matters, but because wanting feels too exposing, too consequential, or too pressurised.  
The source also gives MarriageOS two valuable correctives: self-soothing matters on both sides, and contact must be real before technique helps.  
The final module must not become a therapist lecture, a sex-performance benchmark, or an advanced intimacy workshop.  
It should stay simple, embodied, and non-coercive.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: One or both partners want connection, but wanting itself has become risky. Touch feels loaded. Desire feels pressured or brittle. One partner chases. The other protects.
- likely user state/substate: "I don't even want to want"; "Touch turns into obligation"; "If I say yes, I feel trapped; if I say no, I hurt them."
- what is happening: The relationship is confusing desire with safety. The more important the partner becomes, the more threatening it can feel to want openly without strong self-support.
- what makes it worse: demand for reassurance; reading low desire as indifference; using technique as a substitute for contact; escalating faster than regulation; treating one partner as the problem to solve.
- first corrective principle: lower the pressure and raise the steadiness.
- first behaviour move: create one low-pressure contact window where the goal is not sex, orgasm, or reassurance but calmer presence and honest noticing.
- script logic: support language like "I think wanting has started to feel risky between us. I want to make contact safer, not more loaded."
- tonight move: ten minutes of non-sexual contact or embrace, with no conversion pressure, while noticing tension and staying kind.
- reset logic: regulate first, reduce pursuit-retreat reflexes, protect "no" from punishment, and rebuild contact before novelty or explicit sexual problem-solving.
- escalation boundary: if there is coercion, contempt, fear, trauma activation, or retaliatory withdrawal after refusal, the module must branch outward.

## 6. Claims to preserve

- low desire can mask fear of wanting, not just lack of caring
- fusion and validation-hunger can flatten eroticism
- self-soothing matters on both sides of the loop
- embodied calming and real contact help more than technique alone
- repetitive routine often hides emotional absence
- pressure loops are often anxiety loops

## 7. Claims to soften or translate

### Item 1
- original idea: advanced intimacy exercises as major solutions
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: too intimidating and too explicit for first-response repair
- how it should be translated: start with low-pressure contact and noticing rather than performance tasks

### Item 2
- original idea: low desire is primarily a selfhood problem
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: too narrow; ignores pain, exhaustion, postpartum, medication, trauma, and relational injury
- how it should be translated: selfhood is one major mechanism among several

### Item 3
- original idea: erotic breakthrough language
- why it cannot be carried forward verbatim: risks turning recovery into a benchmark
- how it should be translated: steadier, safer, more real contact is success enough at first
