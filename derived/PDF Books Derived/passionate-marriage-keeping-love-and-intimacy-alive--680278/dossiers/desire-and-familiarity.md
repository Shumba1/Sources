## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: A couple still cares, but desire has become flat, brittle, or overly routine.
- likely user state/substate: "We love each other but it feels dead"; "Nothing is terribly wrong, but the spark is gone"; "We can function sexually, but it feels mechanical."
- what is happening: Familiarity does not kill desire by itself. Desire drops when stability turns into overcaution, when wanting feels too risky, when contact gets replaced by routine, and when partners rely on technique instead of being more honestly present.
- what makes it worse: validation hunger; fear of disapproval; suppressing erotic difference; using routine as safety; treating good sex as a youth privilege.
- corrective principle: selfhood and closeness must grow together if desire is to stay alive.
- related guide(s): guides/read-both
- related repair(s): repair/pressure-loop; repair/quiet-drift

## 6. Claims to preserve

- desire is not just hormones or drive
- false closeness can flatten eroticism
- mature sex is more about who the people are than technique alone
- wanting a partner can feel risky when the partner matters a lot
- routine can protect a bond while quietly de-eroticising it
- being seen and staying present are part of mature intimacy
