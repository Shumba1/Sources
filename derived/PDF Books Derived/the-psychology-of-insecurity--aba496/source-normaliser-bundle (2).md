# Source normaliser bundle

## 1) Source posture

This chapter is a theory-and-evidence piece about the **social-safety system**.
Its strongest contribution is not a communication script or a therapy protocol.
Its strength is naming a repeatable pattern:
- people depend on multiple relational worlds at once
- unexpected behaviour in one world can unsettle felt safety in general
- people often restore stability by looking for safety in another world

## 2) MarriageOS posture conversion

Translate source ideas into MarriageOS language as follows:

- **social-safety system** -> “the mind’s move to find steadiness somewhere”
- **outcome-dependence** -> “how much this situation really affects me”
- **existential uncertainty** -> “I no longer feel sure I understand what is happening”
- **personal relational world** -> “home, partner, family, close circle”
- **sociopolitical relational world** -> “the wider social world: community, institutions, public life”
- **unexpected behaviour** -> “out-of-character, confusing, hard-to-place behaviour”

## 3) Hard translation constraints

Do not carry the following into MarriageOS content without qualification:
- partisan framing or current-politics signalling
- advice that people should trust blindly
- advice that relationship safety is mainly cognitive rather than behavioural
- claims that uncertainty is always bad or always useful
- claims that one partner must function as an unlimited stabiliser

## 4) Content opportunities

### Strong fits
1. Repair guidance for restoring steadiness after a surprising moment
2. Knowledge content explaining why outside chaos affects couple safety
3. Repair guidance for making home feel more dependable during wider-world stress

### Weak fits
- products
- ideology-heavy commentary
- anything that treats public distrust as a relationship cure
- content that erases real danger, betrayal, or chronic unreliability

## 5) Reliability notes

- Strongest source value lies in the mechanism, not the political examples
- The chapter supports a careful idea of relational buffering under uncertainty
- The chapter supports the idea that safety can be strengthened through connection
- The chapter does **not** support denial, magical thinking, or staying in harmful situations
