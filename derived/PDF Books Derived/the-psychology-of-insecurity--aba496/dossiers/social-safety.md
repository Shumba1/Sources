# Dossier: social-safety

## Proposed MarriageOS target
- **Slug:** social-safety
- **Type:** knowledge
- **Status:** strongest conceptual fit

## Why this target
This chapter’s clearest contribution is a usable model: people seek safety across more than one relational world at once. That makes it highly suitable for a knowledge asset explaining why outside instability can alter couple dynamics and why relationship shocks can spill into wider trust.

## Core source signals
- People depend on both close relationships and wider social systems.
- Safety rises and falls with how much a situation matters and how predictable others feel.
- Unexpected behaviour can destabilise not just one bond, but felt safety more broadly.
- People often restore steadiness by leaning psychologically on another relational world.

## MarriageOS translation
Best working proposition:
> When safety drops in one part of your social world, your mind starts scanning for steadiness somewhere else.

## Recommended final shape
A short knowledge piece that explains:
1. the two relational worlds
2. why surprise feels bigger under dependency
3. how stress spills across domains
4. a caution against globalising one confusing moment into “nothing is safe”

## What to avoid
- political commentary
- treating the model as proven destiny
- telling people to override real evidence of harm

## Cautions
- This is a pattern, not a rule.
- Felt safety should not replace behavioural evidence.
- The piece should distinguish everyday uncertainty from abuse or chronic unreliability.
