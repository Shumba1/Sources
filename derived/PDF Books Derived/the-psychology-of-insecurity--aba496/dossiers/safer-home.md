# Dossier: safer-home

## Proposed MarriageOS target
- **Slug:** safer-home
- **Type:** repair
- **Status:** strongest practical repair fit

## Why this target
The chapter suggests that wider-world instability can make people look to close relationships for steadiness. Initial evidence also suggests that being able to find more safety in a marriage during unpredictable periods may help strengthen trust over time.

## Core source signals
- Wider uncertainty can heighten the need to count on others.
- In those moments, people may experience home and partner bonds as a psychological buffer.
- For some less-secure newlyweds, finding more safety in the marriage during external unpredictability was protective over time.

## MarriageOS translation
This dossier should become a calm repair piece about making home feel more dependable when the outside world is noisy, unstable, or frightening.

Best working proposition:
> When the world feels volatile, couples do better when home becomes more predictable, not more chaotic.

## Recommended practical moves
- reduce surprises where possible
- name the outside stress rather than misplacing it onto the partner
- increase visible reliability
- create one or two daily rituals of steadiness
- ask what would feel grounding tonight

## What to avoid
- implying the partner should be an emotional bunker for everything
- promising that rituals erase legitimate structural stress
- confusing safety with avoidance of hard conversations

## Cautions
- The goal is steadiness, not emotional perfection.
- Safety comes from repeated dependable behaviour.
- This piece should stay modest and behavioural.
