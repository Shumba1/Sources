# Dossier: surprise-reset

## Proposed MarriageOS target
- **Slug:** surprise-reset
- **Type:** repair
- **Status:** strong secondary repair fit

## Why this target
A central chapter claim is that unexpected behaviour in a high-dependence situation can make people question whether they understand the other person or even the situation itself. That is highly translatable into a repair asset.

## Core source signals
- Surprise is more threatening when a lot is riding on the moment.
- Unexpected behaviour can trigger broader uncertainty, not just local confusion.
- People often seek safety fast, which can lead to overgeneralising the threat.

## MarriageOS translation
This dossier should become a compact reset guide for the first 10 minutes after an out-of-character moment.

Best working proposition:
> After a surprising moment, repair starts by restoring clarity before assigning meaning.

## Recommended practical moves
- pause before building a global story
- identify the exact surprising behaviour
- ask for context before making motive claims
- separate this event from the whole relationship
- restate one dependable fact before the next conversation

## What to avoid
- telling people to minimise betrayal
- overusing “benefit of the doubt” language in high-risk contexts
- pretending clarification removes all hurt

## Cautions
- This guide is for confusing moments, not dangerous patterns.
- Clarity is not the same as instant trust.
