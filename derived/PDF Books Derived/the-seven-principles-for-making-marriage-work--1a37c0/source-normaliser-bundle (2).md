- dreams-within-conflict and shared meaning

### Caution
- predictive-stat language and questionnaire scoring
- some dated heterosexual and gendered framing
- anything that sounds diagnostic because of one ratio or one conversation
- workshopy or lab-authority voice
- any implication that “nice conflict technique” alone saves a marriage

### Ignore or reject
- bulk workbook material and long questionnaires as product UX
- scorekeeping applications of bids, repairs, or positivity
- importing all research figures verbatim as if they are universally transportable
- any overconfident implication that divorce can be forecast like a certainty

## 6. Dossier recommendations

### 1. quiet-drift
Why strong:
- the source gives unusually clean mechanism language for how relationships thin out
- it shows that drift often begins outside the fight, in missed bids and out-of-date love maps
- it gives practical daily repair without becoming fluffy if translated well

### 2. we-keep-fighting
Why strong:
- this source is canonical-level on fight anatomy
- it provides vivid language for harsh start-up, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling, flooding, and repair failure
- it gives direct script logic through softened start-up and accepting influence

### 3. gridlock
Why strong:
- it distinguishes “same issue, same wound, same loop” from ordinary solvable problems
- it explains why repeated conflict is often identity-loaded
- it offers a strong MarriageOS bridge from winning to understanding

## 7. Normaliser judgment

This source should be treated as a MarriageOS backbone source.

It is not the last word on attachment, trauma, abuse, or sexuality.
It is also not best used as a workbook import.

Its best use is to supply:
- clear conflict mechanism language
- durable anti-drift infrastructure
- explanation for why friendship and admiration are not “soft extras”
- a gridlock frame that moves couples from futile victory-seeking toward deeper understanding
