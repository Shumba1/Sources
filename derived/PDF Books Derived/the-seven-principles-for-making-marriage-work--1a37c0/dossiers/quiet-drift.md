## 2. Target fit summary

This source is a very strong quiet-drift feeder because it treats friendship as the load-bearing beam of a marriage rather than as a sentimental extra.
It explains that connection decays in tiny missed moments, not only in major betrayals or dramatic arguments.
The strongest transferable layers are love maps, admiration, turning toward, and daily alliance rituals.
It should not become a chirpy romance workbook.
Its value is that it gives MarriageOS a disciplined explanation for why drift feels invisible until it feels serious.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the relationship works on paper but feels underfed, flat, or operational
- likely user state/substate: “we are teammates but not really close”; “nothing is exploding, but something is fading”
- what is happening: the bond is thinning through missed bids, low curiosity, low admiration, and lives lived side-by-side rather than together
- what makes it worse: taking each other for granted; thinking romance requires a grand reset; letting work or life stress eat every transition point
- first corrective principle: restore small connection before demanding big emotional transformation
- first behaviour move: one real check-in today and one visible turning-toward move
- script logic: support language like “I do not think we are failing. I think we are underfed.”
- tonight move: short no-logistics conversation and one question about the other person’s inner world
- reset logic: one week of curiosity, appreciation, bid-catching, and daily alliance
- escalation boundary: if the flatness is actually contempt, fear, active secrecy, or loaded touch, route elsewhere
