## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the couple has the same destructive loop and feels like every hard conversation goes off the rails
- likely user state/substate: “we cannot talk without it turning ugly”; “one comes in hot, the other shuts down”; “we never actually solve anything”
- what is happening: criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and shutdown are outpacing repair and pushing both people into threat mode
- what makes it worse: starting with accusation; trying to prove the other person is the problem; ignoring overwhelm; treating repair as weakness
- first corrective principle: lower the temperature of entry and stop trying to win the opening minute
- first behaviour move: softened start-up plus one repair phrase both people will recognize
- script logic: support language like “I know I play some part in this. I want to talk about one specific thing, how it lands for me, and what would help.”
- tonight move: identify your most common fight pattern and agree on one repair phrase and one pause rule
- reset logic: one week of softer entry, responsibility language, overwhelm recognition, and repair
- escalation boundary: if contempt is chronic, if one partner refuses all influence, or if there is coercion or fear, route outward

## 6. Claims to preserve

- how you start matters
- criticism is not the same as complaint
- contempt is especially destructive
- shutdown often reflects flooding
- repair attempts matter
- accepting influence makes compromise possible
