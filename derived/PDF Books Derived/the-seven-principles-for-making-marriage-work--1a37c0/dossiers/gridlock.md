## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the couple has the same fight again and again and each compromise feels like betrayal
- likely user state/substate: “we never get anywhere on this”; “it always comes back”; “if I give in, I lose something core”
- what is happening: this is likely not just a task problem; the conflict is carrying identity, fear, history, values, or life dream energy
- what makes it worse: trying to fully solve a perpetual difference; arguing only at the topic level; treating the partner as obstacle rather than as holder of a meaning you do not yet understand
- first corrective principle: stop trying to eliminate the difference; understand the meaning underneath it
- first behaviour move: each person names what this issue represents, protects, or threatens
- script logic: support language like “I think this issue means more to each of us than we have been saying. I want to understand what it touches in you before we push for a solution.”
- tonight move: identify whether the issue is solvable, perpetual but manageable, or a real deal-breaker
- reset logic: one week of meaning-level questions, lower-total-win pressure, and one ritual of reconnection outside the conflict
- escalation boundary: if the issue is truly a deal-breaker—children, abuse, untreated addiction, repeated betrayal, incompatible life plan—this module must not overpromise

## 6. Claims to preserve

- not every recurring conflict is fully solvable
- compromise can feel impossible when identity or values are attached
- gridlock often hides deeper dreams or fears
- understanding matters more than total victory
- shared meaning helps couples carry enduring differences without constant collapse
