# Pressure Loop

Sex often stops feeling easy when it becomes the place where the relationship’s distance, rejection pain, shame, and unmet needs all get acted out.

## Best used when…

- One of you is pursuing sex and the other is withdrawing from it.
- Sexual rejection is becoming highly painful or highly loaded.
- One of you feels unwanted while the other feels pressured, depleted, or shut down.
- Talking about sex quickly turns into blame, silence, or despair.

## Not for…

- sexual coercion
- trauma activation that is not being handled safely
- humiliation, threats, or punishment around sex
- active addiction or betrayal that is still destabilising the bond
- any situation where “repair” is really a cover for pressure

## What’s Happening

Sex is not separate from the relationship climate.

When the bond is strained:

- one partner may pursue sex to feel chosen, wanted, or reassured
- the other may pull away because sex feels loaded, pressured, resentful, or simply too costly
- each side then confirms the other’s fear

The pursuer feels:
- unwanted
- rejected
- lonely

The withdrawer feels:
- pressured
- misunderstood
- ashamed
- overwhelmed
- emotionally far away

The more this repeats, the harder it is to talk about sex without both people bracing.

## What Makes It Worse

These patterns usually deepen the loop:

- discussing sex only in the moment of yes or no
- treating withdrawal as proof of no attraction, with no further curiosity
- treating pursuit as selfishness, with no attention to the hurt underneath it
- pushing for sex when what is missing is validation, responsiveness, or rest
- leaving resentment, trauma, or exhaustion unnamed
- making one partner carry all the sexual responsibility

## Do This First

Move the conversation out of the charged moment.

Do not try to solve your sex life in the middle of rejection pain.

Instead say:

> I do not want us to only talk about this when one of us is hurt.  
> I think sex has become the place where our distance shows up.  
> I want us to understand the pattern, not pressure each other harder.

## Say This

For the sexual pursuer:

> When we do not connect physically, I can start telling myself I do not matter to you.  
> I know that may not be the whole story, but that is what it feels like inside me.

For the withdrawing partner:

> When sex feels loaded, I pull away even more.  
> That does not always mean I do not want you.  
> Sometimes it means I feel overwhelmed, disconnected, pressured, or ashamed.

## Do This Tonight

Do not make tonight about sex.

Make it about safety.

Try this:

- sit close if that feels okay
- no initiation attempt
- no post-mortem on every past rejection
- ask one question each:
  - What makes closeness feel easier for you?
  - What makes sex feel harder for you right now?

The goal is understanding.  
Not conversion.

## 7-Day Reset

### Day 1
Agree that sex will not be discussed only in the middle of a charged moment.

### Day 2
Add one piece of non-demand affection.

### Day 3
Name one valid factor that may be getting in the way: overwhelm, resentment, parenting depletion, rejection history, low self-care, medical factors, trauma, or shame.

### Day 4
Validate the pain on the other side of the pattern.

### Day 5
Have one attachment-friendly conversation about what helps each of you feel close.

### Day 6
Reduce one source of avoidable pressure.

### Day 7
Review whether the bond feels even slightly safer than it did a week ago.

## Escalate If…

Get stronger support if:

- sex feels coercive or unsafe
- trauma is clearly in the room
- addiction is destabilising trust
- shame and avoidance are so high that no conversation can happen
- one partner is using anger, pressure, or punishment around sexual connection
