## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the couple keeps having the same argument, but never really resolves the deeper injury underneath it
- likely user state/substate: “We keep getting pulled into the same fight”; “One of us pushes and the other shuts down”; “We both feel alone when it is over”
- what is happening: the content topic is real, but the fight is being driven by unmet attachment needs, triggers, and protective stances
- what makes it worse: criticism, blame, defensiveness, invalidation, shutting down, trying to solve the issue before restoring safety
- first corrective principle: stop treating the partner as the enemy; treat the cycle as the enemy
- first behaviour move: spot the trigger early, pause, and interrupt the familiar pattern before it escalates
- script logic: the user needs language for “I think we are in our cycle again. Underneath this I am hurting and trying to protect myself.”
- tonight move: one short repair attempt focused on naming experience, not solving the whole issue
- reset logic: map each partner’s trigger sequence, reduce negative-cycle entry, and practise one small repair daily
- escalation boundary: if the pattern involves fear, coercion, intimidation, active addiction, unresolved betrayal, or escalating contempt, branch out

## 6. Claims to preserve

- the issue being argued about is often not the deepest problem
- protective stances make sense but block closeness
- the negative cycle is the real enemy
- one partner can interrupt the cycle
- repair must restore safety before problem-solving
- vulnerability is choosing a new response rather than the familiar protection
