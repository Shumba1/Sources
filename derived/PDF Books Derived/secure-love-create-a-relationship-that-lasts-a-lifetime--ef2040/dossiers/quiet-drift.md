## 2. Target fit summary

This source is not as playful or behavioural as other anti-drift books, but it gives a stronger explanation for why drift feels so heavy.
Its contribution is the climate logic: unrepaired misses, low responsiveness, and chronic coexistence change the felt environment of the relationship.
That is ideal for MarriageOS because many couples in quiet drift do not need inspiration first—they need a better explanation of what went wrong.
It should not become a long attachment lecture.
Its job is to explain flatness accurately and then get the user back into small repairable moves.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: the couple is not necessarily exploding, but they no longer feel reliably close, comforted, or emotionally reachable to each other
- likely user state/substate: “We coexist”; “We keep the house going but do not feel bonded”; “We are fine until we are suddenly not”
- what is happening: small unrepaired ruptures, low responsiveness, and low emotional tending have changed the attachment climate of the relationship
- what makes it worse: minimising little misses, telling yourself distance is normal now, relying only on logistics, and skipping repair because nothing seems “big enough” to warrant it
- first corrective principle: drift is often cumulative underfeeding, so repair has to become cumulative refuelling
- first behaviour move: name one unmet closeness need and create one deliberate moment of responsiveness today
- script logic: support language like “I don’t think we are empty. I think we have too many unrepaired misses and not enough felt connection.”
- tonight move: one short check-in built around comfort and understanding rather than fixing
- reset logic: seven days of better noticing, one daily response-to-need, and one explicit small repair
- escalation boundary: if the distance covers contempt, coercion, active betrayal, or fear, branch out

## 6. Claims to preserve

- security is a felt experience
- little ruptures matter
- climate matters more than isolated weather
- closeness requires responsiveness and validation
- quiet coexistence can still be painful and repair-worthy
- repair is an ongoing process, not a single talk
