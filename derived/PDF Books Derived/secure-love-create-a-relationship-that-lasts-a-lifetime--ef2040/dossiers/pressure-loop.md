## 2. Target fit summary

This source does not provide the most exciting or erotic material in the batch, but it gives something more useful for MarriageOS: a strong explanation for why sex gets shut down when the attachment climate is bad.
Its direct fit is not generic libido advice. Its direct fit is sexual pursue / withdraw, rejection pain, emotional neglect, resentment, depletion, and trauma-sensitive caution.
It should not become a sex-help manual or a libido taxonomy.
Its job is to make the user see that sex is often collateral damage from the bond, and that repairing the bond changes the conditions for desire.

## 5. Core content logic

- problem pattern: sex has become a pursue / withdraw zone, with one partner experiencing rejection pain and the other experiencing pressure, shame, depletion, or emotional distance
- likely user state/substate: “I feel unwanted”; “I feel pressured”; “It is easier to avoid sex than talk about it”; “Every conversation about it goes badly”
- what is happening: the sexual relationship is tracking the attachment climate of the couple; unresolved distance and negative cycles are showing up in the bedroom
- what makes it worse: treating sex as an isolated yes/no issue, taking every withdrawal as global rejection, using disappointment as pressure, ignoring resentment or overwhelm, discussing sex only inside the charged moment
- first corrective principle: move the problem out of the bedroom and out of the immediate yes/no frame
- first behaviour move: name the pattern without blame and restore one nonsexual moment of safety
- script logic: support language like “I think sex has become the place where our distance shows up. I want us to understand the pattern, not pressure each other harder.”
- tonight move: no demand, no fixing, just one conversation about what blocks closeness and what helps it
- reset logic: one week of non-demand affection, reduced rejection interpretation, self-care, and one attachment-friendly sex conversation
- escalation boundary: if there is coercion, trauma activation, active addiction, humiliation, or fear, branch out immediately

## 6. Claims to preserve

- sexual connection is vulnerable and bond-sensitive
